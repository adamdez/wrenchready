### Why Customers Choose Mobile Over a Shop
- No tow bill, no waiting room, no ride coordination, no time off work
- Service happens where the vehicle already is
- Photo-backed findings + plain-language recommendations

## Revenue Model

### Year-One Scenarios
| Case | Gross Revenue | Take-Home | Billable Hrs/Day | Avg Rate |
|------|-------------|-----------|-----------------|----------|
| Base | ~$212K | ~$102K | 4.5 | $120/hr |
| Target | ~$325K | ~$158K | 5.5 | $135/hr |
| Breakout | ~$407K | ~$196K | 6.0 | $140/hr |

### Revenue Formula
Revenue = (Billable Hours/Day × Avg Labor Rate × Working Days) + Parts Markup + Diagnostic Fees + Trip Fees

### Key Economics
- Oil change ARO ladder: $85 base → $140–$175 blended through inspection-driven add-ons
- Diagnostic fee: $150 flat, credited toward repair, 90%+ margin
- Parts markup: 25–70% tiered by visibility (batteries low, hardware high)
- Fleet potential: One 8-truck landscaping company = ~$11K/year recurring

## Trust & Conversion Framework

### The Inspection-Led Model
Every maintenance visit includes an 18-point inspection. Findings presented as:
- **NOW** — Safety issues, likely-to-fail items (top 1–3 only)
- **SOON** — Address within 30–90 days
- **MONITOR** — Document and revisit

### Evidence-Based Recommendations
- Battery test printout showing CCA/health
- Brake pad measurement in mm
- Tread depth gauge readings
- Dirty filter shown next to clean replacement
- Brake fluid moisture test strip
- NO blanket flush selling, NO pressure, NO unsubstantiated urgency

### Revenue Guardrails (Non-Negotiable)
- Never pressure customers into unnecessary work
- Never present every finding as urgent
- Never bury customers in 10 recommendations at once
- Never use manipulative urgency language
- Protect long-term trust over same-day revenue

## Risk Awareness (From Pre-Mortem)

### Top Failure Modes
1. Owner burnout from uncapped hours
2. Van failure (single point of failure)
3. Inspection process perceived as sales theater
4. Review velocity below trust threshold
5. Weak calendar design / route scatter
6. Parts-margin compression on visible items
7. First hire destroys trust before systems catch it
8. Spokane winter crushes both productivity AND demand
9. Deferred work logged but never followed up
10. Revenue grows without GP/route-hour growing

### Kill Metrics (4+ weeks = stop and fix)
- Avg travel time between jobs >25 min
- ARO not rising after inspections implemented
- Owner spending >3 hrs/day on admin by month 6
- Review velocity <4 new reviews/month
- Deferred work conversion <25%
- Revenue growing without GP/route-hour increasing
