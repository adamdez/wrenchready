# WrenchReady Prompt Answers

This is the working brief behind the current WrenchReady site pass.

The goal is simple:
- make the site sound like a real Spokane mobile service business
- make the offer easier to trust
- make the service mix clearer
- make the SEO stronger without writing for Google instead of people

## 1. Service Mix Profit Audit

### Demand wedge
- Battery replacement
- Brake service
- Check engine diagnostics
- Pre-purchase inspections

### Core profit drivers
- Battery replacement
- Brake service
- Paid diagnostics

### Selective only
- Oil changes as routine maintenance, repeat-customer work, or route-fill work

### Add-on only
- Small maintenance or inspection extras that naturally fit an existing visit

### Avoid leading with
- Cheap everything-menu promises
- Low-ticket jobs that break the route
- Anything that sounds like "we do every repair anywhere"

### What the site should market first
- No-start help
- Battery replacement
- Brake service
- Paid diagnostics
- Pre-purchase inspections

### What should never lead the marketing
- Oil changes as the main promise
- Generic "mobile mechanic for everything" language

## 2. Quote Script Builder

The site should sound like this when it quotes:

- calm
- clear
- not greasy
- not salesy

Best pattern:
1. Confirm the symptom or service.
2. Explain the first step.
3. Explain the starting price or pricing logic.
4. Explain what happens after booking.
5. Ask for the vehicle and address.

Best site implication:
- every major service page should explain the first step clearly
- diagnostics should be framed as a real first answer, not a throwaway fee
- battery and brake pages should explain exactly what is included

## 3. Bundle Finder

Most natural add-ons:

- Battery replacement -> charging-system check, terminal cleanup, next-step note if starter/alternator is suspect
- Brake job -> tire and brake wear note, rear brake status, fluid note if relevant
- Oil change -> quick inspection, upcoming maintenance note, second household vehicle offer
- Pre-purchase inspection -> post-purchase baseline service or deferred-work planning
- Diagnostics -> approved repair or clear referral/next step

Best site implication:
- add-on language should feel practical, never pushy
- the site should talk more about "clear next step" than "upsell"

## 4. Route Density Planner

Best operating rule:
- strong-fit jobs first
- grouped geography second
- low-value or ambiguous jobs last

Keep:
- batteries
- brakes
- diagnostics
- inspections
- grouped maintenance

Move or decline:
- vague low-ticket requests far outside the route
- jobs with bad parking, unclear scope, or unrealistic timing

Best site implication:
- keep the service area honest
- explain why screening exists
- never promise county-wide instant service just to sound bigger

## 5. Fleet Outreach Builder

Best early B2B targets:
- small contractor fleets
- landscapers
- property managers
- apartment maintenance teams
- delivery/service vans

Value proposition:
- less downtime
- no shop shuffle
- clear paperwork and next-step notes
- practical recurring maintenance relationship

Best site implication:
- the public site does not need a giant fleet section yet
- but it should sound organized enough that a fleet owner would not bounce

## 6. Review Mining Prompt

What people actually value in this category:
- showing up when promised
- clear explanation
- not replacing parts blindly
- being treated fairly
- convenience without chaos

What makes people suspicious:
- vague pricing
- "probably this part" guessing
- no explanation
- surprise add-ons
- vague arrival windows

Best words to lean into:
- clear
- honest
- showed me
- explained
- on time
- easy
- straightforward

Best site implication:
- trust language should focus on clarity, proof, and follow-through
- not hype, not swagger, not "best mechanic in Spokane"

## 7. Local SEO Prompt

Best money pages:
- mobile mechanic Spokane
- battery replacement Spokane
- mobile brake repair Spokane
- check engine diagnostics Spokane
- pre-purchase inspection Spokane

Best support pages:
- service area pages
- neighborhood pages
- FAQ blocks tied to real objections
- "what happens after you book" style trust content

Top FAQ topics:
- what jobs are a good mobile fit
- whether diagnostics are credited
- whether the customer has to know the repair first
- where the car can be parked
- how soon a visit can happen

Best SEO rule:
- write the page for the customer first
- keep metadata, structure, and internal links clean
- never let public copy sound like an SEO plan

## 8. Retention System Builder

The real long-term win is not one job.
It is the next job.

Best follow-up sequence idea:
- same day: what we did and what comes next
- 7 day: check-in if there was deferred work
- 30 day: maintenance or follow-up reminder if relevant
- seasonal: battery, brake, oil, and inspection reminders based on prior service

Best site implication:
- keep talking about "clear next step"
- make repeat service feel organized, not accidental

## 9. Dispatcher Decision Prompt

Best decision logic:
- book now if the job is clear, local, safe, and high fit
- require paid diagnostic if the customer knows the symptom but not the repair
- quote later if the job needs more info
- bundle only if the job is low value by itself
- decline if the fit is bad and the promise will be shaky

Best site implication:
- the booking flow should feel like screening, not desperate lead capture
- the customer should understand why more information helps

## 10. Weekly Operator Prompt

The five highest-leverage moves for the business right now are:

1. Build the public trust layer honestly.
Reason:
The site is strong visually, but the trust story is still thin without reviews and visit standards.

2. Lead harder with the best service lanes.
Reason:
Battery, brakes, diagnostics, and inspections are the strongest fit and the clearest local demand wedges.

3. Strip internal SEO/operator language off public pages.
Reason:
That language weakens trust and makes the brand sound artificial.

4. Make the booking path feel safer.
Reason:
Customers need to know what happens after they submit, what gets screened, and how quickly they will hear back.

5. Turn every completed visit into the next visit.
Reason:
Repeat business, deferred work, and review capture are where the real compounding starts.

## Site Changes This Brief Calls For

- Remove review schema until real reviews exist.
- Replace weak "reviews are coming" filler with honest launch-stage trust proof.
- Rewrite service, location, and FAQ support copy so it sounds customer-facing.
- Keep battery, brake, diagnostics, and inspections in the lead lane.
- Keep oil changes clearly positioned as support-lane work, not the whole brand.
